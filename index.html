I am a Ph.D candidate in the Department of Computer Science and Engineering at the University of North Texas, advised by Prof. Hyunsook Do. My research interests lie in the area of requirements engineering with an emphasize on software maintenance and evolution.

In my research, I primarily focus on quality-related bug detection problems. Alongside this focus, I develop tools to enhance and contextualize the understanding of complex quality issues that emerge as by-products of long-term maintenance efforts, while consistently seeking to promote effective methodologies and strategies for improving software quality. Meanwhile, I actively engage in projects centered around code summarization techniques that aim to provide a holistic understanding of functional aspects of software systems.

Prior to joining the Ph.D. program at the University of North Texas, I taught Computer Science at various institutions, covering undergraduate and graduate-level courses. I also worked as a senior Java developer for over three years, contributing to the development of various web-based Java applications. Additionally, I used advanced Oracle technologies to create complex SQL reports for a large enterprise resource planning project.

I hold a B.S. degree in Computer Science and Mathematics from the University of Prishtina (Kosovo) and an M.S. degree in Computer Science and Engineering from the University of Notre Dame.
